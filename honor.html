Honorable Mentions

Short highlights of 2 games that are worth talking about.

Sonic Colors

Genre: Action Platformer

Info: Released in 2010 for the Nintendo Wii and Nintendo DS by SEGA

What is it?: Sonic Colors is a fast-paced platformer where you play as Sonic the Hedgehog trying to stop Dr. Eggman from using an interstellar amusement park to capture and control alien creatures called Wisps. The game focuses on high-speed running, quick reactions, and colorful environments, mixing 2D and 3D sections as you dash through each level.


The stages are split into different themed areas of Eggman's space park, like a tropical resort, a candy-themed world, a rollercoaster-like asteroid zone, and more. Each area has multiple acts, with lots of alternate paths and secrets. A big feature is the Wisps: special power-ups that give Sonic abilities like turning into a laser to zip through lines of rings, drilling through the ground, or rocketing straight up. These mechanics encourage replaying levels to find new routes and collectables.


I like Sonic Colors because it's bright, fun, and satisfying when you're boosting through levels and chaining Wisp powers together. The music and visuals give it a really happy, energetic vibe. It didn't make my top 6, though, because while I enjoy it a lot, I feel more attached to other games that have deeper stories, more replay value, or stronger memories for me. So it's still a game I think is great, just more of an honorable mention compared to my absolute favorites.

Mario Party 9

Genre: Fun Party Game

Info: Released in 2012 for the Nintendo Wii by Nintendo

What is it?: Mario Party 9 is a party game where you play mini-games and move around a game board with Mario characters like Mario, Luigi, Peach, Yoshi, and more. Instead of everyone moving separately like older Mario Party games, all players ride together in a vehicle, taking turns as the captain and trying to collect the most Mini Stars by the end of the board.


The stages are different themed boards, like a grassy starter area, a spooky haunted board, a lava-filled volcano, and a space-themed map. Each board has its own events, hazards, and boss battles where everyone works together to fight a big enemy but still competes for points. The game includes lots of mini-games that test things like timing, luck, and quick reactions, and there are special spaces on the board that can help you, hurt you, or steal stars from other players. The shared car mechanic and boss fights are the big features that make Mario Party 9 feel different from the earlier games.


I like Mario Party 9 because it's fun, looks colorful, and the mini-games are a good mix of simple and competitive, so it's easy to play with friends or family. The boss battles and the way everyone rides in the same vehicle keep the game moving and create funny, chaotic moments. It didn't make my top 6, though, because I still prefer the older Mario Party style where everyone moves on their own and collects regular Stars, and some of those games feel more classic and memorable to me. So Mario Party 9 is still an enjoyable party game I'm glad I played, but it fits better as an honorable mention.
