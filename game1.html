Splatoon 3

Genre: Third-Person Shooter

Why I like it:

Splatoon 3 is a fast-paced third-person shooter where players use ink instead of bullets, and that twist is what makes it feel completely different from other games. You're not just trying to eliminate players, you're trying to cover the map with your team's color, move through your own ink faster, and outplay opponents using creative weapons like rollers, dualies, and chargers. The game is fun because every match feels chaotic and energetic, and no two battles play out the same way. The movement, the colors, the style, and the constant back-and-forth for map control make the game feel alive, and it builds that “one more match” feeling every time you play.


I also play Splatoon 3 competitively, which adds another level of excitement to the game. In comp, you're not just playing for fun, you're analyzing maps, learning team compositions, working on positioning, and practicing callouts with teammates. Every mistake matters, every push matters, and every big play feels way more rewarding. Playing competitively pushes me to understand the game deeper, especially things like weapon matchups, specials, and rotations. It's also a great feeling when all the practice pays off and you clutch a team fight or make the right decision in overtime.


The story modes in Splatoon 3 add even more to the experience. The main story, “Return of the Mammalians,” lets you explore different test-like levels while uncovering what's happening with the Octarians and the sudden return of fuzzy enemies. It mixes platforming, puzzle-like challenges, and boss fights in a way that teaches you new mechanics without feeling like a tutorial. The Side Order DLC focuses on a different style of gameplay, a roguelike run where you climb the Spire of Order, power up your character with color chips, and try to break out of a washed-out, colorless world. Both modes show different sides of Splatoon's universe and give you something fun to do outside of multiplayer while still keeping the same style and creativity the series is known for.
